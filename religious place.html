Religious Places in Kurukshetra

Shri Sthaneshwar Mahadev Temple

This ancient Shiva temple is believed to have been visited by Lord Krishna and the Pandavas before the battle of Mahabharata.

Birla Mandir

A beautiful modern temple built by the Birla family, dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha, offering peaceful gardens and spiritual vibes.

Bhadrakali Temple

One of the 51 Shakti Peethas, where the anklet of Goddess Sati is said to have fallen. Devotees offer terracotta horses as prayer symbols.

6th Patshahi Gurudwara

A significant Sikh shrine where Guru Hargobind Sahib Ji is believed to have stayed during his travels. It's a peaceful and spiritual place for visitors.

Venkateshwara Temple

A replica of the famous Venkateshwara Temple in Tirupati, this temple in Kurukshetra is dedicated to Lord Venkateshwara (Balaji) and attracts devotees seeking his divine grace.

Baba Kali Kamli Temple

Dedicated to Baba Kali Kamli, a revered saint, this temple is known for its serene atmosphere and the blessings of inner peace and spiritual guidance.

Jairam Vidyapeeth

An educational and spiritual hub, this temple honors Lord Rama and promotes values of knowledge and devotion, blending spirituality with learning.

Bhishm Kund, Narkantari

A sacred spot where it is believed that Bhishma Pitamah, lying on his bed of arrows, drank water from the kund to quench his thirst during the Mahabharata battle.
